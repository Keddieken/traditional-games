Sansankroma

Ghana

Sansankroma is a popular and well-known Akan folk song and children's game from Ghana. The name "Sansankroma" translates to "Sansa the Hawk." The song tells the story of an orphaned hawk named Sansa who, without a mother or father to care for him, resorts to snatching baby chicks to survive. However, the song's deeper meaning and the moral of the story are rooted in the strong sense of community within Akan culture. It serves as a reminder that if a child were to become an orphan, they would not be left to fend for themselves; instead, they would be cared for by a relative or the entire village. The song is often accompanied by a stone-passing game, where children sit in a circle and pass rocks or other objects to a specific rhythm, making it both a fun activity and a way to teach children about communal responsibility and empathy

Diketo

South Africa

A traditional South African game involving stones, testing hand-eye coordination and speed.

Morabaraba

South Africa

A classic South African board game of strategy, also known as "Twelve Men's Morris", played with 12 tokens per player. Players aim to form mills and capture opponent's pieces.

Ncuva

South Africa

A traditional South African board game from the Mancala family, played with pebbles and holes in the ground. Players move pieces to capture the opponent's stones using strategy and planning.

Kgati

South Africa

A traditional South African skipping game played with a long rope, usually by girls in groups, to develop rhythm, coordination, and fitness.

Mhele

Botswana

A traditional Botswana board game similar to checkers, played with stones or seeds on a drawn board, requiring strategy and planning.

Matalane Mokgatla

Botswana

A hide-and-seek style game played by children in many Botswanan communities, encouraging teamwork and agility.
